## 1. Executive Summary

**Melo is the first parenting app built for two parents.** Where existing
apps target the mother and treat the father as a passive observer, Melo
gives each parent their own dashboard, content, and tools — and connects
them through a shared layer of tasks, appreciation, and real-time
communication.

We launch in the Danish market in mid-2026, with a clear path to Nordic
expansion within 12 months and broader European rollout within 24.

**Why this matters now.** Modern parents are sharing the load more
equitably than any generation before them — but the tools they reach for
were built for a different family model. Mental load research shows that
67% of couples experience significant decline in relationship satisfaction
in the first year of parenthood; equitable task sharing is the strongest
predictor of recovery. Melo turns that research into product.

**Status (May 2026):** the app is feature-complete for launch. Pregnancy
week-by-week content, sleep tracking, shared diary, partner sync,
AI-powered chat, full dad-side experience for both pregnancy and the
post-birth phase, GDPR-compliant data architecture, and privacy policy
in place. Clinical content is in final review with a Danish midwife.

## 2. Company

**Melo Parents ApS**, founded 2026, headquartered in Denmark.

**Mission:** make parenting more equitable by giving fathers and
co-parents an app that treats them as primary, not peripheral, and by
giving couples the small daily rituals that protect the relationship
through the hardest year of their lives.

**Vision:** to be the calm hand that walks alongside every new family in
Europe through pregnancy and the first year — without ads, without dark
patterns, and without selling user data.

---

## 3. The Problem

**Three overlapping problems we solve:**

**a) The father gap.** Pregnancy and parenting content is overwhelmingly
written for mothers. Fathers and co-parents open these apps, find nothing
written for them, and stop opening them. Research consistently shows that
fathers who are engaged from day one have stronger bonds for life — but
the tools don't help them get there. The Danish parental leave system has
shifted dramatically toward equal sharing (mandatory 11 weeks earmarked
for each parent from 2022); the product layer hasn't caught up.

**b) The mental load gap.** Even when fathers are engaged, the mental
work of running a household with a baby — knowing what needs doing,
remembering appointments, planning meals — falls disproportionately on
mothers. This invisible labor is a leading driver of postpartum
depression and relationship breakdown.

**c) The trust gap.** The current pregnancy app market is dominated by
ad-supported and data-monetized models. Vulnerable, sleep-deprived
parents are sold formula, supplements, and lifestyle products at scale.
The space lacks an option built around clinical integrity and privacy.

---

## 4. The Solution

Melo is one app with **role-aware experiences** for both parents, joined
by a real-time collaboration layer.

**For the pregnant or birthing parent:** week-by-week content verified
against Danish health authority guidance, nutrition and supplement
tracking, sleep tracker, postpartum recovery support, shared diary, and
warm, non-judgmental reminders about her own well-being.

**For the father / co-parent:** a parallel dashboard with content
written in a language he relates to (kettlebell weight comparisons,
workout-framed activities, achievement-unlocked framing), concrete ways
to support the mother (varying by feeding method and stage), age-aware
play ideas, and small daily missions.

**For the couple:** a "take" mechanic where one parent can take a task
from the other with one tap, paired with an appreciation flow where the
recipient can send a small ❤️🥹😮 reaction back. Shared needs ("I need
air / a nap / a meal / a hug"), a date-night dream feature where each
partner can leave ideas for the other, and an AI chat that helps
navigate everyday questions.

**Privacy and clinical integrity by design:** all data sits in Supabase
EU region, protected by Row Level Security so no family ever sees
another's data. No ads. No third-party tracking. All clinical content
signed off by a Danish midwife. AI chat does not train on user data.

## 5. Market Opportunity

### Target user

Denmark has approximately **60,000 births per year** [verify against
Danmarks Statistik]. Add Sweden (~110,000), Norway (~52,000), and Finland
(~46,000) for a Nordic TAM of **~268,000 new births annually** — and a
two-parent reach of **~500,000+ active parent-app users annually**, given
each pregnancy typically involves two installed apps.

Expanding into Western Europe (Germany, Netherlands, UK, France, Spain)
opens a TAM of **~3.5 million births per year**, or **~6 million
addressable parent-app installs annually**.

### Spend per family

Industry benchmarks suggest active pregnancy/baby app users spend
**€30–€60/year** on app subscriptions and adjacent digital products. At
mid-range pricing (€40/year), the Nordic SAM is **~€10–20M annual
revenue**; the European SAM is **~€120–240M**.

### Why this market is favorable

- Digital-native parents now make up the majority of new births in Europe.
- Two-parent app installs are the new norm (industry data shows ~70% of
  fathers install the pregnancy app their partner uses — but stop opening
  it within 4 weeks).
- The Nordic countries have the most generous and most equal parental
  leave policies in the world, which means the father-as-primary-user
  hypothesis is testable in our home market.

## 6. Business Model

**Freemium with family subscription.** Free tier covers the core app —
week-by-week, sleep tracker, diary, partner sync, basic chat. Premium
unlocks expanded content, expert deep-dives, unlimited AI chat,
downloadable formats (birth plan, hospital checklist), and priority
support.

### Pricing (Denmark, year 1)

- **Monthly:** 39 DKK (~€5.20)
- **Annual:** 299 DKK (~€40), positioned as 25 DKK/month
- **Pregnancy bundle:** 499 DKK one-time, valid 24 months

One subscription covers both parents in the same family. This is both a
fairness signal (the app only works if both can use it) and a virality
mechanic.

### Adjacent revenue streams (Year 2+)

- **Affiliate / curated commerce** through the existing wishlist feature
  — partnerships with Babysam, Bayba, Natalys, apotek for vitamin
  supplements
- **Premium courses** — birth preparation, sleep, breastfeeding — at
  99–299 DKK each
- **B2B / employer benefit packages** — pension and insurance companies
  (PFA, Pensiondanmark, Tryg, Alka) offering Melo as part of family
  well-being benefits, and municipal pilots with health-visitor services

## 7. Go-to-Market Strategy

**Phase 1 — Launch (months 0–6):** soft launch in Denmark, free for the
first 3–6 months to build base and gather user feedback. Acquisition
through three channels:

1. **Clinical endorsement** — a named Danish midwife signs the content
   and lends her name to launch. Endorsement appears in App Store
   listing, marketing, and in the app itself. Establishes trust signal
   from day one.
2. **Partner-of-Mødrehjælpen** — a non-commercial partnership giving
   Melo distribution and credibility halo.
3. **Organic content + PR** — founder-led content on LinkedIn and
   parenting podcasts; pitches to relevant Danish media (Politiken
   familie-tillæg, DR's Borger.dk, Femina, Alt for Damerne).

**Phase 2 — Monetize (months 6–12):** premium subscription introduced
based on what early users actually use. Pilot with a Danish kommune for
B2B validation (we have early conversations with [PLACEHOLDER]).

**Phase 3 — Nordic expansion (months 12–24):** Swedish and Norwegian
localization. Existing brand and product translate directly. Acquisition
follows the same playbook through local clinical endorsement.

**Phase 4 — Europe (months 24+):** Germany and Netherlands first
(largest birth volumes, similar regulatory environment, high willingness
to pay for digital well-being products).

| Competitor | Region | Model | Differentiation gap for Melo |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Preglife** | SE/DK/NO | Freemium subscription (~39 SEK/mo) | Mom-focused content. Dads ignored. |
| **Flo** | Global | Freemium subscription (~€7/mo) | Cycle-tracking origin, weak partner features. |
| **BabyCenter** | Global | Ad-supported | Heavy on advertising; trust issues. |
| **Ovia** | USA | B2B / employer benefit | Not present in EU consumer market. |
| **Peanut** | Global | Social, freemium | Community-driven, not couple-focused. |
| **Glow** | Global | Freemium subscription | Cycle-first; no postpartum depth. |

**Melo's moat:** the two-parent design. It is structurally hard to copy
because it requires a deep content investment for fathers, a
collaboration layer that competitors don't have, and a brand promise
(no ads, no manipulation) that contradicts the dominant business model.

We are not competing for the same user attention — we are reaching the
50% of the household who current apps don't serve at all.
